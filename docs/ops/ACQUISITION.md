# goose.gifts Acquisition and Link-Earning Plan

This is the durable plan for earning qualified referral traffic and legitimate
links. It is not a mandate to drop links, automate replies, mass-email people,
or submit the site to low-quality directories.

## Objective

Earn attention by creating destinations and content that are independently
useful, entertaining, or citeable. Optimize for qualified referring domains,
referral sessions, on-site searches, and outbound product clicks—not raw link
count.

## Credibility gate

Before proactive outreach or community promotion:

- the homepage must show consistently funny, strange, relevant products;
- representative guides must be distinct enough to deserve indexing;
- stale legacy search results must redirect cleanly to useful destinations;
- the promoted page must have a clear author/source, update date, methodology
  where applicable, affiliate disclosure, and a worthwhile mobile experience;
- the pitch must point to a specific useful asset, not the generic homepage.

## Opportunity order

### 1. Build a link-worthy Weird Gift Index

Create a recurring editorial/data asset such as **The Weird Gift Index: What
3,000 Ridiculous Products Reveal About Gifting**. A seasonal first edition can
focus on white elephant and office exchanges.

Candidate material:

- dominant humor and product archetypes;
- strange category combinations;
- click concentration by product type;
- recipient and occasion patterns;
- seasonal changes;
- price-availability or budget patterns, with limitations stated clearly;
- a documented weirdness/relevance methodology;
- original charts and quote-ready findings.

Do not bulk-republish retailer images, imply that stale prices are current, or
present generated classifications as objective facts without explaining the
method.

**2026-07-12 implementation:** `/weird-gift-index` analyzes 3,275 active
catalog listings using published merchant-title dictionaries. It includes a
named byline, limitations, aggregate JSON, Article/Dataset/FAQ schema, a social
image, correction contact, and explicit affiliate disclosure. This is the first
asset to carry into the acquisition queue; new adjacent Index pages should wait
for actual citation, referral, and reader evidence.

### 2. Build a random ridiculous gift utility

Create a polished route that shows one excellent product at a time, supports a
fast “another one” interaction, has stable share URLs and custom OG imagery,
and optionally filters by recipient, budget, or chaos level. It must use the
same hard relevance gate as the homepage.

This becomes a useful destination for social posts, newsletters, community
answers, embeds, and the Weird Gift Index. Any embedded attribution link should
be unobtrusive and `nofollow`, not keyword-rich distributed link spam.

### 3. Targeted editorial and newsletter outreach

After an asset passes the credibility gate, build a small prospect set across:

- gift-guide and holiday editors;
- humor, internet-culture, and unusual-product writers;
- workplace and office-culture newsletters;
- design publications when the asset has genuine design or data value;
- independent Substack curators;
- product creators included in an original report.

Every pitch should identify one relevant finding or visual and explain why it
fits that outlet. Send small personalized batches and follow up at most once.
Posting or sending remains approval-gated.

### 4. Native social series

Prepare channel-native units rather than a repeated link feed:

- one beautifully art-directed “object of the day” plus a sharp sentence;
- “would you bring this to a white elephant?” polls;
- short visual category threads;
- honest creative rejects and what failed the taste gate;
- original Weird Gift Index findings.

X is a possible first channel after account/API access and an approved content
format. Bluesky is a lower-cost secondary test. Queueing drafts can be
autonomous; account creation, posting, replies, and API spend are not.

### 5. Publisher and community distribution

- Add original editorial posts, a named byline/contact surface, and RSS before
  evaluating Flipboard publisher distribution.
- Use reactive-PR services only when goose.gifts has defensible data or genuine
  expertise relevant to a journalist request.
- Participate in communities only after reading current rules. Answer useful
  questions without a link when that is the better contribution, disclose the
  connection when linking, and never automate posts, votes, replies, or DMs.

## Avoid

- purchased or exchanged ranking links;
- bulk directories and low-quality guest-post networks;
- automated comment/forum links;
- mass outreach or scraped-email blasts;
- fake personas, engagement, reviews, or votes;
- browser-scripted social posting outside supported APIs;
- repeated cross-platform duplicates with no native value.

Paid placements must use appropriate `rel="sponsored"` or `nofollow`
qualification. Affiliate recommendations and social posts must carry clear FTC
and Amazon Associate disclosures close to the recommendation.

## Acquisition goal workflow

```text
Goal: Earn qualified referral traffic and legitimate editorial links to
goose.gifts without spam, paid-link schemes, or deceptive promotion.

Each cycle:
1. Read live search/indexation, referral, click, and catalog-quality signals.
2. Verify that the proposed landing page passes the credibility gate.
3. Choose one acquisition hypothesis and one specific audience.
4. Build or materially improve one link-worthy asset.
5. Discover and score relevant channels or prospects.
6. Prepare unique posts or outreach drafts with tracking parameters.
7. Stop for approval before account creation, posting, sending, or spending.
8. After authorization, execute through supported APIs or a documented manual
   workflow.
9. Measure after 7, 30, and 60 days.
10. Record the result, rejection reason, and next hypothesis.
```

Suggested durable ledgers:

- acquisition assets and credibility-gate status;
- prospects/outlets, fit, and last-contact state;
- outbound draft queue;
- earned links and referral traffic;
- experiment decisions in `docs/ops/MARKETING.md`.

The operational queue, current prospect research, primary-source policy notes,
and approval-gated draft examples live in `docs/ops/acquisition/README.md`.
Every queue item is internal-only until the exact draft and route receive owner
approval; research or drafting never authorizes an external action.

Score prospects using topical fit, real audience, editorial quality, pitch
specificity, likely referral value, and effort. Never score by domain authority
alone.
